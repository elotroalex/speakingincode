Speaking in Code

Speaking in Code will be an NEH-supported summit for digital humanities software developers, dedicated to voicing the things we tacitly embody in our platforms and tools: expert knowledge about the intellectual work of code-craft and unspoken understandings about its relation to scholarly method and humanities theory. In the coming academic year, the Scholars' Lab will help the DH developers' community devise concrete, pragmatic initiatives to address the following question—in its own terms: "How can we bring technical conversations that may strike traditionally-trained scholars as too informal, inaccessible, or telegraphic into more open, inclusive humanities discourse?"

Watch this space or follow us for details.

Speaking in Code is a project of the Scholars’ Lab at the University of Virginia Library, generously supported by the National Endowment for the Humanities.
